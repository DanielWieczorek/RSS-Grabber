Overall Architecture by Section

The overall structure can be seen in the diagram below. The microservices can be categorized into the following sections: GUI , Data Collection , Data Processing and Trading

Each service may only perform REST calls on microservices in the same or an earlier section. E.g. "rss-insight" may use any service from the sections Data Processing and Data Collection . This enforces an information flow from Data Collection over Data Processing to the Trading services.

The GUI sections is an exception from this. It has access to all microservices to visualize them.

Overall Architecture by Streams

You can see the architecture from a functional perspective as well. From this perspective the architeture is organized in streams. Each stream is concerned with reading and processing the data from a particular type of source e.g. RSS feeds or chart data.

Data Collection

rss-reader

Reads the RSS feeds from various crypto coin news sites and stores them in its DB. The entries are made availables via a REST API. Special effort was made to ensure that adding new sites is as simple as possible and the corresponding classes are detected automatically via Weld mechanisms.

chart-reader

Reads the OHLCV data from Kraken and stores them in its DB. The data is made availables via a REST API.

Processing

rss-classification

Loads the RSS entries from rss-datacollection and stores them in its DB. It offers REST services such that a user can classify the entries. this service basically generates the training data for other services.

rss-insight

Automatically classifies the entries read by rss-reader and stores the classified entries in its DB. For this one Word2Vec network is used to generate word vectors and then an RNN is applied to classify whole sentences. The training data is requested from rss-classification.

Trading

rss-advisor

Creates a prediction by how much the exchange rate between BTC and EUR will move up or down in the next 15 minutes. For this the chart data of the last 24 hours and the classified rss-entries are read by an RNN which then generates a prediction. In the training all points in time are used where we have the actual exchange rate of this point in time + 15 minutes.

trader

The trading service uses the predictions of the advisors of the last 2 hours and makes a decision to buy or sell every 10 minutes. N-step Q-learing is used to learn the best 19 consecutive trade decisions for a time period. This is used to avoid bad decisions, just because the exchange rate is fluctuating. For this a tree with the observation (current account value, exchange rates of the last two hours, predictions of advisors) as vertices and the decisions as edges is created.
